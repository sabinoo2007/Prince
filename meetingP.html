✨ The First Meeting

A Small Voice

You smile at the boa. Suddenly, a golden-haired child stands before you. He does not explain himself. He only asks, in a voice quiet as starlight:

"If something is invisible, does it mean it does not exist?"

How do you answer?

"If I can't measure it, it isn't real." "Some things are felt, not seen." "Define 'exist' first — philosophically speaking."
📏

❌ Rational Pride

The Little Prince nods — politely, sadly — and turns away. He has met many grown-ups like you. Logic is useful. But it cannot measure love, or loneliness, or the sound a star makes when you laugh beside it.

"It is only with the heart that one can see rightly."

❌ Intellectual Vanity

The child waited while you philosophized. Then he picked a flower and walked away. Some questions are not meant to be dissected. They are meant to be lived.
