Planet III · The Sin of Gluttony

The Tippler

The air is thick with the smell of old corks. A man sits in silence among mountains of empty bottles. The Tippler drinks to forget the shame of drinking.

He poses you a riddle. Answer it:

"What feeds on itself and grows stronger the more you hide it?"
A) Fear B) Hunger C) Shame D) Greed

Planet III · Your Response

He Looks at You

"Yes. Shame. And yet — knowing does not stop the drinking."

He offers you a glass of "Mist," promising it will make the desert memories disappear. What do you do?

A) Sit with him and share a drink. B) Lecture him about the dangers of alcohol. C) Sit silently beside him — then ask: "What hurts?"
🍾

❌ You Forget Why You Came

The wrong answer. The tippler shook his head and poured himself another. You have not understood shame — only its surface. You begin to forget why you came.

❌ Lost in Forgetting

You joined him. Hours passed. Then days. You forgot the rose, the fox, the Little Prince. You forgot what you were searching for.

"He is the only person I think of as ridiculous — and yet he is less ridiculous than all the rest."

❌ Cold Judgment

He stopped listening after your third word. People drowning in shame do not need a lecture — they need someone brave enough to ask what hurts without flinching.
