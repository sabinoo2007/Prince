Planet VI · The Geographer

Anger & Lust for Knowledge

You arrive on the final planet. A man maps mountains and oceans he has never seen, obsessed with perfect knowledge. You feel a flicker of envy. Here, everything is certain. Stay, and you would never feel lost again.

The Geographer senses your hunger. He poses his first riddle to see if you are worthy of a seat in his silent library:

"What can be possessed only by letting it go?"
A) Power B) Knowledge C) Love D) Time

Planet VI · Final Question

"What is most important on Earth? Tell me, traveler."

The Geographer freezes. He slowly writes your answer into his great book with a heavy, scratching sound.

He leans in, posing a second question—not as a test, but as a plea from a man who has forgotten how to live:

"Tell me, traveler. Of everything on Earth — what is truly essential?"
A) Power — the ability to shape events and people. B) Mountains — the permanence of great things. C) One fragile rose — something that needs you, and whom you need.
